Privacy Policy — Rummage

Effective date: July 15, 2026

Rummage is a single-operator content tool. It creates short videos and publishes them to the operator's own TikTok and Instagram accounts using the platforms' official APIs. It is not offered to the public, has no users other than its operator, and provides no login, signup, or data-submission surface of any kind.

What data the app accesses

Through the TikTok Content Posting API and the Instagram Graph API, the app accesses only the operator's own accounts: it uploads videos and captions the operator's system generated, and reads back basic performance statistics (view, like, comment, and share counts) for those same posts.

What data the app stores

OAuth access and refresh tokens for the operator's accounts, plus the post metadata and statistics described above, are stored locally on the operator's own computer. Nothing is stored in the cloud, and nothing is transmitted anywhere except to TikTok and Meta through their official APIs.

What the app does not do

  • It does not collect, store, or process any data about any person other than its operator.
  • It does not sell, share, or transfer data to third parties.
  • It does not use tracking, advertising, or analytics services.
  • It does not message, follow, comment, or interact with other accounts.

AI-generated content

All content the app publishes is AI-generated and is labeled as such, both in captions and via the platforms' AI-content disclosure flags.

Data deletion

To revoke the app's access, remove it in TikTok's Settings → Security → Apps or Meta's Business integrations settings; locally stored tokens can be deleted at any time by the operator. Contact the operator at nate.g.hawkins@gmail.com for anything else.
